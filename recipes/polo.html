Polo

Description

Polo (a Central Asian variant on the word pilaf) is a staple accross Central Asia including Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Uzbekistan, Turkmenistan, Tajikistan, and parts of North Western China. The different peoples of the region have their own takes, and flavours, for the recipe.

This recipe is for Uyghur polo, the variant found in China's Xinjiang Uyghur Autonomous region. Uyghur food is on the whole closer to Turkish, Persian or Mediterranean food than it is to conventional Chinese food. The recipe can be a challenge to cook correctly at first, but the challenge will prove well worth it because the dish is absolutely delicious.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup oil
  • 1lb beef or lamb
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 1 carrot, julienned
  • salt, as you prefer
  • 2 1/4 cups of water
  • 2 cups of rice
  • 1 bulb of garlic
  • 1 red hot pepper
  • 1/2 teaspoon of cumin
  • 1/2 a cup of raisins (optional)

Steps
  1. Heat oil in a large pot on a high heat over the stove
  2. Cook the meat in the oil, stirring occasionally until evenly browned
  3. Add the chopped onion to the pot, and cook until translucent
  4. Add the carrots into the pot and stir
  5. Add salt according to your taste
  6. Pour water into the pot
  7. When the water boils, add the pepper and garlic. After 5 minutes, take them back out of the pot and set aside
  8. Add cumin
  9. Lower the stove temperature and allow to boil for 15 - 20 minutes
  10. Raise the heat again and add rice to the pot. Spread the rice out above the water, but do not mix it with the rest of the ingredients.
  11. When the water is no longer visible above the rice, then spoon the rice toward the centre
  12. Place the pepper and garlic bulb back into the mixture, and pour raisins(optional) on top of the rice
  13. Place lid on top of the pot and lower the temperature
  14. Cook for 30 minutes. After that time, remove the pepper and garlic, and stir the rest of the ingredients. This dish is often served with flatbread, yoghurt and salad
